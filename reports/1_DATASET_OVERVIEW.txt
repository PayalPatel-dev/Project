PROJECT: Multimodal Patient Deterioration Prediction System
OBJECTIVE: Predict patient clinical deterioration using vital signs + clinical notes
DATASET: MIMIC-IV (Medical Information Mart for Intensive Care)

================================================================================
1.1 DATA SOURCES
================================================================================

SOURCE 1: VITAL SIGNS DATA
├─ Origin: MIMIC-IV Database
├─ Patient Population: ICU patients
├─ Time Resolution: Hourly measurements
├─ Duration Per Patient: 24-hour window
├─ Total Features: 6 vital sign measurements
└─ Quality: High (clinical-grade monitoring)

VITAL SIGN FEATURES (6 dimensions):
  1. Heart Rate (HR)
     - Unit: Beats per minute (bpm)
     - Normal range: 60-100
     - Range in data: 40-180
     - Clinical significance: Indicates cardiac activity

  2. Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP)
     - Unit: Millimeters of mercury (mmHg)
     - Normal range: 100-130
     - Range in data: 60-250
     - Clinical significance: Upper pressure in arteries

  3. Diastolic Blood Pressure (DBP)
     - Unit: Millimeters of mercury (mmHg)
     - Normal range: 60-80
     - Range in data: 30-150
     - Clinical significance: Lower pressure in arteries

  4. Respiratory Rate (RR)
     - Unit: Breaths per minute
     - Normal range: 12-20
     - Range in data: 5-40
     - Clinical significance: Breathing frequency

  5. Oxygen Saturation (SpO2)
     - Unit: Percentage (%)
     - Normal range: 95-100
     - Range in data: 70-100
     - Clinical significance: Blood oxygen percentage

  6. Temperature (Temp)
     - Unit: Celsius (°C)
     - Normal range: 36.5-37.5
     - Range in data: 35-42
     - Clinical significance: Body temperature

SOURCE 2: CLINICAL NOTES DATA
├─ Origin: MIMIC-IV Clinical Documentation
├─ Note Types: Nursing notes, physician assessments
├─ Average Length: 150-300 words per note
├─ Language: English
├─ Format: Free-text narrative
├─ Encoding: SentenceTransformer embeddings (384-dim)
└─ Quality: High (written by healthcare professionals)

SOURCE 3: OUTCOME LABELS
├─ Target Variable: Patient Deterioration (Binary)
├─ Class 0: No deterioration / Stable patient
├─ Class 1: Patient deterioration detected
├─ Label Source: Clinical outcome data from MIMIC-IV
└─ Definition: Based on ICU metrics and outcomes

1.8 DATA LIMITATIONS & CONSIDERATIONS
================================================================================

KNOWN LIMITATIONS:

1. Source Bias
   - Only ICU patients (not general population)
   - May not generalize to other settings
   - Selection bias towards sicker patients

2. Temporal Bias
   - Data from specific time period
   - Medical practices may have changed
   - Equipment/sensors may differ now

3. Geographic Bias
   - Specific hospital system (MIMIC-IV)
   - May not reflect other hospitals
   - Regional healthcare variations

4. Missing Clinical Context
   - Patient medications not included
   - Lab values not in vital signs
   - Diagnostic information limited

5. Label Definition
   - Deterioration defined by ICU metrics
   - May not match other definitions
   - Outcome may vary by hospital
